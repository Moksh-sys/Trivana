About Us

Trivana, a platform dedicated to celebrating the rich heritage of tribal arts and crafts. Our mission is to connect local artisans which are skilled with the world, Showcasing their unique craftsmanship that has been passed down through generations.

We work closely with tribal communities to preserve their traditions, provide fair trade opportunities, and bring authentic handmade products directly to your home.

Every product tells a story — a story of culture, tradition, and creativity. By supporting us, you are empowering artisans and helping preserve cultural legacies for future generations.

Meet Some Tribal Artist

Jangarh Singh Shyam

*Gond Artist

was a pioneering contemporary Indian painter credited with being the creator of a new school of Indian art called Jangarh Kalam

Bhuri Bai

*Bhil Artist

Bhuri Bai is an Indian Bhil artist. She was born in Pitol village, situated on the border of Madhya Pradesh and Gujarat. Pitol is a village of Jhabua district in Madhya Pradesh.

Lado Bai

*Bhil Artist

Lado Bai was born in the Badi Bawadi village of Jhabua, Madhya Pradesh in the Bhil tribal community.At a very early age, she moved to Bhopal with her family to be engaged as manual labourers in the building of the iconic Bharat Bhavan.

Mayank Shyam

*Santhal Artist

Mayank was born in Bhopal in 1987. Mayank is the eldest son of Jangarh Singh Shyam, and learned painting under him from a young age. He has participated in many exhibitions and art camps in India and abroad. Inheriting his father’s talent, Mayank is cultivating his own style, and is also recognised as a contemporary artist. In addition to works based on Gond mythology and perception of nature, he also works on modern themes as an artist who grew up in the city.
